## SOC 2 Structured Review

### Security

Zamp's Vanta-based trust report lists numerous passing infrastructure controls, including "The company's datastores housing sensitive customer data are encrypted at rest" and "The company uses secure data transmission protocols to encrypt confidential and sensitive data when transmitted over public networks" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Access controls are extensive, including "Remote access MFA enforced" and "Production database access restricted" to authorized users with a business need [zamp - controls.pdf]. An incident response plan exists and is tested: "The company tests their incident response plan at least annually" [zamp - controls.pdf], though note that individual control "isPassing" statuses are all null in this export, meaning pass/fail results are not independently confirmable from this document alone.

### Availability

The documentation does not describe an end-user data portal or self-service export/deletion feature within Zamp's own product; this is a B2B tax compliance tool where OXD/TF/ThoughtFarmer staff, not end customers, would manage transaction data submission. The Terms note customer responsibility: "Customer is responsible for providing Company any necessary transaction data by the fifth (5th) of every month" and that upon termination "Company will make all Customer Data available to Customer for electronic retrieval for a period of thirty (30) days" [Terms & Conditions _ Zamp.pdf]. No SLA/uptime commitment is stated in the provided documents.

### Processing Integrity

Zamp processes transaction data to calculate and file sales tax; the Terms state Zamp "will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide Customer the Services" and disclaims responsibility for "tax positions taken by Customer" [Terms & Conditions _ Zamp.pdf]. On AI-assisted processing, the vendor states "AI processing is isolated per customer; one customer's data is never used to serve another customer" and that AI requests route through "Vercel AI Gateway, which operates under a zero-data-retention policy at the gateway layer when enabled" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf]. Audit-related controls exist at the infrastructure level ("Log management utilized"), but no customer-facing audit trail or usage log feature is described [zamp - controls.pdf].

### Confidentiality

Access to production systems is restricted per role: "The company restricts privileged access to databases to authorized users with a business need" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Data may be shared with subprocessors: "Our primary subprocessors for customer data include AWS (infrastructure/hosting), PlanetScale (database), Vercel (application hosting and AI Gateway), Browserbase, and Inngest" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf]. The Privacy Policy separately discloses broad sharing rights for personal data collected via the marketing website ("With business partners: We may share Your information...to offer You certain products, services or promotions" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf]), which is a materially different (and more permissive) data-sharing posture than the transactional tax data Zamp processes as a service provider — this distinction is a genuine ambiguity in the documentation.

### Privacy

For the actual sales-tax use case, Zamp states purpose limitation: "We will process personal data from transactions (such as shipping addresses, products purchased, and amounts) only to the extent necessary to provide the US sales tax services specified in the relevant service agreement" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf]. It also states "Zamp does not currently support sales tax calculations for countries outside the US, so international transactions are not needed in our systems" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf], confirming a US-only data scope consistent with the stated use case. However, retention is vaguely defined ("only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf]) and a dedicated deletion policy is unfinished: "Our Data Deletion & Erasure Policy is in progress" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf].

## Approved Uses

- ✔︎ Use Zamp to calculate, register, and file US state sales tax for ThoughtFarmer's own billing transactions.
- ✔︎ Submit US customer billing data (name, address, transaction amount) to Zamp strictly for sales tax calculation and filing purposes.
- ✔︎ Use Zamp's API integration to automate monthly transaction data submission for tax compliance.
- ✔︎ Rely on Zamp for US multi-state sales tax registration as ThoughtFarmer's US footprint expands.

## Restrictions

- Do not submit Canadian, UK, or EU client, citizen, or employee PII to Zamp, as it is scoped and documented for US-only tax data.
- Do not use Zamp to process any OXD public-sector client or citizen data — restrict use to ThoughtFarmer's internal US sales tax administration only.
- Report this tool to the AI Policy Oversight team for formal review and addition to the Internal AI Tool Directory before continued use, since it is not currently listed as pre-approved for PII.
- Have finance staff manually verify all Zamp-calculated tax filings before submission, consistent with the vendor's own disclaimer that it does not warrant results.
- Revisit this assessment once Zamp's SOC 2 Type II report and Data Deletion & Erasure Policy are finalized.
