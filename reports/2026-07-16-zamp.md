## SOC 2 Structured Review

### Security

Zamp's Vanta trust report lists extensive infrastructure controls including encryption, access restriction, and monitoring, e.g. "The company's datastores housing sensitive customer data are encrypted at rest" and "The company uses secure data transmission protocols to encrypt confidential and sensitive data when transmitted over public networks" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Access controls include "Remote access MFA enforced" and "Production database access restricted" to authorized users with a business need [zamp - controls.pdf]. An incident response plan is documented and tested: "The company tests their incident response plan at least annually" and maintains "security and privacy incident response policies and procedures that are documented and communicated to authorized users" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Note these controls reflect Vanta-monitored control claims; the underlying SOC 2 Type II report itself is still "in progress" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf].

### Availability

The Terms & Conditions give customers a right to their data on termination: "Upon any termination, Company will make all Customer Data available to Customer for electronic retrieval for a period of thirty (30) days" [Terms & Conditions _ Zamp.pdf]. Customer responsibilities are defined around providing accurate monthly transaction data and maintaining funds for remittance: "Customer is responsible for providing Company any necessary transaction data by the fifth (5th) of every month" and "maintaining sufficient funds in his bank account to pay his sales tax obligation" [Terms & Conditions _ Zamp.pdf]. No specific uptime SLA percentage is stated in the provided documentation.

### Processing Integrity

Data is used to calculate and file sales tax: "We require specific data to accurately calculate and file sales tax with US states" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf], and the Terms note Zamp's warranty to provide services "with the degree of skill and care reasonably expected from a skilled and experienced supplier" [Terms & Conditions _ Zamp.pdf]. Control self-assessments are conducted: "The company performs control self-assessments at least annually to gain assurance that controls are in place and operating effectively" [zamp - controls.pdf]. No explicit customer-facing audit-trail/log feature for transaction-level review is described in the documentation provided; this is a gap the assessment notes rather than assumes.

### Confidentiality

Access to data is restricted internally via role-based and need-based controls: "The company restricts privileged access to databases to authorized users with a business need" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Data may be shared with service providers, affiliates, and business partners per the Privacy Policy: "We may share Your personal information with Service Providers... With Affiliates... With business partners" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf]. Subprocessors are named for infrastructure: "Our primary subprocessors for customer data include AWS (infrastructure/hosting), PlanetScale (database), Vercel (application hosting and AI Gateway), Browserbase, and Inngest" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf], and data in transit is encrypted per the controls document.

### Privacy

Zamp collects PII including "Email address, First name and last name, Phone number, Address, State, Province, ZIP/Postal code, City" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf] and retains it "only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf], though no specific retention period is given. Legally, Zamp positions the customer as data controller: "Under GDPR, we believe the data controller, not the processor, is responsible for the data submitted or filtered to Zamp" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf], and scopes its processing narrowly: "We will process personal data from transactions... only to the extent necessary to provide the US sales tax services" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf]. Zamp confirms it does not handle non-US transactions: "Zamp does not currently support sales tax calculations for countries outside the US, so international transactions are not needed in our systems" [Privacy Policy _ Zamp.pdf].

## Approved Uses

- ✔︎ Use Zamp to calculate, register for, and file US state sales tax obligations for ThoughtFarmer's US-based operations.
- ✔︎ Submit US customer transaction data (order amounts, shipping addresses, products purchased) needed strictly for sales tax calculation and filing.
- ✔︎ Use Zamp's dashboard/API integration for monthly transaction data uploads per the standard filing cadence.
- ✔︎ Rely on Zamp's automated CSV parsing feature for structuring transaction data, given its documented per-customer isolation.

## Restrictions

- Limit data submitted to Zamp to the minimum transaction fields necessary for tax calculation and filing.
- Do not submit Canadian, UK, or EU client or citizen PII through Zamp given its US-only scope and undefined data residency.
- Finance staff must review and verify Zamp-generated tax filings before submission to state authorities.
- Track Zamp's SOC 2 Type II report completion and request the report under NDA once issued, per the AI Policy Oversight team's new-feature reporting practice.
- Report any new AI-driven features Zamp introduces to the AI Policy Oversight team before relying on them for client-impacting work.
