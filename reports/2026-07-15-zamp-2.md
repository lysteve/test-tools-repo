## SOC 2 Structured Review

### Security

Zamp reports encryption of sensitive data both at rest and in transit, per its Vanta-monitored controls: "The company's datastores housing sensitive customer data are encrypted at rest" and "The company uses secure data transmission protocols to encrypt confidential and sensitive data when transmitted over public networks" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Access controls, network segmentation, MFA-enforced remote access, intrusion detection, and log management are also listed as implemented controls, e.g. "Remote access MFA enforced" and "Network segmentation implemented" [zamp - controls.pdf]. An incident response plan exists and is tested: "The company tests their incident response plan at least annually" and maintains "security and privacy incident response policies and procedures that are documented and communicated to authorized users" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Note that these controls are self-attested via the Vanta trust center with individual pass/fail status shown as "null" (not independently verified in the supplied documentation), and formal SOC 2 Type 2 attestation is not yet complete — "Our SOC 2 Type II examination is in progress" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf] with the engagement letter noting the audit "having started on April 20, 2026" [engagement-letter.pdf].

### Availability

Customers retain ownership and some self-service control over their submitted data: "Customer shall own all right, title and interest in and to the Customer Data" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf], and on termination "Company will make all Customer Data available to Customer for electronic retrieval for a period of thirty (30) days" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf]. Customer responsibilities are explicit and significant given the tax-filing use case: "Customer is responsible for providing Company any necessary transaction data by the fifth (5th) of every month" and "Customer is responsible for maintaining sufficient funds in his bank account to pay his sales tax obligation" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf]. Customers are also responsible for equipment, credentials, and account security: "Customer shall be responsible for maintaining the security of the Equipment, Customer account, passwords... and for all uses of Customer account or the Equipment with or without Customer's knowledge or consent" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf].

### Processing Integrity

Data is processed specifically to calculate and file US sales tax: "We require specific data to accurately calculate and file sales tax with US states" [Privacy Policy | Zamp.pdf], and Zamp acts under a limited power of attorney to file returns with state agencies: "Customer grants Company a limited power of attorney, and appoints Company as Customer's attorney-in-fact and agent, to access such third-party sites" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf]. Zamp's warranty is limited to service performance per an "Accuracy Guarantee" but explicitly disclaims verification of customer-supplied data: "Company may not audit or otherwise verify any information customer provides" [Terms & Conditions | Zamp.pdf]. Formal usage/audit-trail logging for Thoughtfarmer-relevant transaction processing is referenced only generally via "Log management utilized" and "Infrastructure performance monitored" controls [zamp - controls.pdf]; a customer-facing audit trail for individual filings is not described in the documentation, so this is an uncertainty rather than a confirmed gap given the low sensitivity of tax transaction data.

### Confidentiality

Access to production systems and data is restricted to authorized personnel with documented procedures: "The company restricts privileged access to databases to authorized users with a business need" and "Production application access restricted" [zamp - controls.pdf]. Subprocessors handling customer data are named: "Our primary subprocessors for customer data include AWS (infrastructure/hosting), PlanetScale (database), Vercel (application hosting and AI Gateway), Browserbase, and Inngest" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf], and the Privacy Policy notes broader potential sharing categories (affiliates, business partners, business transfers) that are standard boilerplate but broader than the tax-filing use case strictly requires [Privacy Policy | Zamp.pdf]. AI-based processing (e.g., CSV parsing) is stated to be customer-isolated and routed through a zero-retention gateway: "AI processing is isolated per customer; one customer's data is never used to serve another customer" and prompts/outputs "are permanently deleted after each request, not retained by Vercel, and not used to train Vercel's own models" [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf], though "provider-level zero-data-retention enforcement" across all downstream model providers is still being evaluated, leaving a residual gap for full-chain confidentiality assurance [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf].

### Privacy

Zamp collects identifiable contact information ("Email address, First name and last name, Phone number, Address...") and transaction data needed for tax filing, retaining Personal Data "only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy" or as legally required [Privacy Policy | Zamp.pdf]. For GDPR-relevant customers, Zamp positions itself as processor and the customer as controller, limiting scope to US filings: "Under GDPR, we believe the data controller, not the processor, is responsible for the data submitted... Zamp does not currently support sales tax calculations for countries outside the US, so international transactions are not needed in our systems" [Privacy Policy | Zamp.pdf]. This aligns well with the stated usage context (US-only tax compliance for Thoughtfarmer's US clients), meaning Canadian/EU/UK data residency concerns are largely inapplicable to this tool's actual use; however, the policy's broad marketing/sharing clauses and lack of a finalized Data Deletion & Erasure Policy ("in progress" per [Zamp Security Questions - Answers.pdf]) are process gaps worth noting for any employee or business contact PII incidentally processed.

## Approved Uses

- ✔︎ Calculate, register, and file US multi-state sales tax obligations for ThoughtFarmer's US-based clients.
- ✔︎ Submit US client transaction data (amounts, addresses, products) via dashboard or API for automated tax determination.
- ✔︎ Provide US business contact details (name, email, phone, address) needed solely to complete state tax registrations and filings.
- ✔︎ Use Zamp's CSV parsing feature to structure US transaction data ahead of filing submissions.
- ✔︎ Retrieve and export filed US tax records within the 30-day post-termination window when offboarding the tool.

## Restrictions

- Do not submit Canadian, UK, or EU personal or transaction data into Zamp, as the tool is scoped and intended for US-only tax compliance.
- Do not use Zamp for any government, citizen, or public-sector client data given its incomplete SOC 2 Type 2 attestation and unverified controls.
- Limit data entry to the minimum contact and transaction information required for US tax filing, avoiding any broader PII categories.
- Flag this tool to the AI Policy Oversight team for formal review given its pending SOC 2 Type 2 completion and evolving AI feature set.
- Manually verify all tax calculations and filings before relying on them, since Zamp disclaims verification of customer-supplied data.
